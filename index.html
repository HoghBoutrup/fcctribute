Marie Curie

Marie Sklodowska Curie (Born: Marie Salomea Sklodowska) was born in Warsaw in 1867. She started har studies in Warsaw until 1891 where she at age 24 followed her sister Bronislawa to study in Paris. In 1895 she married the french physicist Pierre Curie. In 1903 she shared the 1903 Nobel prize in physics with her husband and with the physicist Henri Becquerel for their work developing the theory of "radioactivity" The 1911 Nobel prize in Chemistry was also won by Marie Curie for her discovery of the elements polonium and radium.

Legacy

  • The first ever woman to win a Nobel Prize
  • The first person to win the Nobel Prize more than once
  • The Element Curium is named after Marie and her husband Pierre
  • Her daughter Irine Joliot-Curie (pictured) won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry with her husband in 1935
